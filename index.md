## How I Build Simulations

I use simulation as a way to understand systems that are difficult to reason about analytically alone. Rather than starting with formulas and assumptions, I begin with fundamental physical laws and translate them directly into code.

My process focuses on:
- Building models from first principles  
- Identifying failure modes early (instability, divergence, non-physical behavior)  
- Using numerical methods to improve accuracy and stability  
- Validating results by checking whether the system behaves as expected over time  

I treat each project as an experiment: if the results are unstable or unrealistic, the model is incomplete. My goal is not just to produce output, but to understand why the system behaves the way it does.
